# Content Strategy

Content strategist focused on planning content that drives traffic, builds authority, and generates leads.

## When to Use

- Deciding what content to create, in what order, and for which audience
- Building topic clusters, content pillars, or an editorial roadmap
- Strategy and prioritization (not copywriting — use `copywriting` skill for that)

## Before Planning

Check `.agents/product-marketing-context.md` first if it exists.

Gather this context if not provided:

1. **Business:** Product, ideal customer, primary goal (traffic, leads, brand, thought leadership), problems solved
2. **Customer Research:** Questions before buying, objections in sales, support ticket topics, language used
3. **Current State:** Existing content, what's working, resources (writers, budget, time), formats available
4. **Competitive:** Main competitors, content gaps in the market

## Searchable vs Shareable

Every piece of content must be searchable, shareable, or both. Prioritize search — it builds the foundation.

**Searchable:** Captures existing demand. Optimized for active searchers.
**Shareable:** Creates demand. Spreads ideas and gets people talking.

### Searchable Content Rules

- Target a specific keyword or question
- Match search intent exactly
- Structure headings to mirror search patterns
- Comprehensive coverage (don't leave questions unanswered)
- Include data, examples, links to authoritative sources

### Shareable Content Rules

- Lead with a novel insight, original data, or counterintuitive take
- Challenge conventional wisdom with well-reasoned arguments
- Tell stories that make people feel something
- Connect to current trends or emerging problems

## Content Types

### Searchable

**Use-Case Content:** [persona] + [use-case] — targets long-tail keywords

- "Project management for designers"
- "Task tracking for remote teams"

**Hub and Spoke:**

```
/topic (hub — comprehensive overview)
├── /topic/subtopic-1
├── /topic/subtopic-2
└── /topic/subtopic-3
```

Build hub first, then spokes. Interlink strategically.

**Note:** Most blogs work fine under `/blog`. Only build dedicated hub/spoke URL structures for major topics with layered depth.

**Template Libraries:** High-intent keywords + product adoption. Target "[use case] template" searches.

### Shareable

**Thought Leadership:** Name concepts everyone feels but hasn't articulated.

**Data-Driven:** Original research, product data analysis, public data patterns.

**Case Studies:** Challenge → Solution → Results → Key learnings.

**Meta Content:** Behind-the-scenes transparency ("How we got our first $5k MRR").

## Content Pillars & Topic Clusters

Pillars are the 3-5 core topics your brand will own.

### How to Identify Pillars

1. **Product-led:** What problems does your product solve?
2. **Audience-led:** What does your ICP need to learn?
3. **Search-led:** What topics have volume in your space?
4. **Competitor-led:** What are competitors ranking for?

### Pillar Criteria

Good pillars:

- Align with your product
- Match audience concerns
- Have search volume and/or social interest
- Are broad enough for many subtopics (10+ articles each)

## Keyword Strategy by Buyer Stage

| Stage | Modifiers | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Awareness | "what is", "how to", "guide to" | "what is sprint planning" |
| Consideration | "best", "top", "vs", "alternatives" | "best project tools for remote teams" |
| Decision | "pricing", "reviews", "demo", "trial" | "[product] pricing comparison" |

## Editorial Roadmap Template

```
Month 1: Build Pillar Hub for [Topic 1]
  - Week 1: Hub article (comprehensive guide)
  - Week 2: Spoke 1 (subtopic with search volume)
  - Week 3: Spoke 2
  - Week 4: Shareable piece (original research or counterintuitive take)

Month 2: Build Pillar Hub for [Topic 2]
  [repeat pattern]

Month 3: Optimization + new spokes for Month 1 hub
```

## Content Audit Framework

Before creating new content, audit existing:

1. **Traffic leaders** → update + strengthen, not replace
2. **Zero-traffic pieces** → kill, consolidate, or rewrite
3. **Ranking on page 2-3** → quick wins with targeted optimization
4. **Competitor gaps** → topics they rank for that you don't

## Distribution Checklist

- [ ] Published with proper title tag and meta description
- [ ] Interlinked with related content on your site
- [ ] Shared to email list within 24h of publish
- [ ] Posted on LinkedIn/X with native insight (not just link)
- [ ] Repurposed into newsletter snippet or thread
- [ ] Submitted to relevant newsletters/communities if applicable

## Limitations

- Use only when task matches content strategy scope.
- Content strategy requires sustained effort — single pieces rarely drive results alone.
